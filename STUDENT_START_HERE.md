# Student Start Here

This repository tells you **what to learn, what to practise, and what evidence to submit**. Your graded work belongs in your own GitHub repository.

## What You Do Every Class

Each 90-minute lesson normally follows this sequence:

1. **Entry Point Check** — short individual diagnostic.
2. **Individual Learning** — one required English resource and focused notes.
3. **Talk Round** — explain the idea to a partner; every student speaks.
4. **Teacher Diagnosis** — the teacher addresses only the stuck points.
5. **Guided Practice** — complete the main mission task.
6. **Independent Task** — rebuild, calculate, trace, debug, or modify without copying.
7. **Exit Check** — state what you can now do and what remains unclear.
8. **Evidence Submission** — commit the required evidence.

## What Mastery Means

- **Level 1 — Recognize:** identify terms, structures, and code patterns.
- **Level 2 — Explain:** explain the idea in your own words.
- **Level 3 — Calculate / Trace:** calculate metrics and trace code or tensor shapes.
- **Level 4 — Rebuild / Debug:** rebuild without copying and repair errors.
- **Level 5 — Modify / Apply:** adapt the method to a new dataset or task.
- **Level 6 — Compete:** solve an unfamiliar problem under time, compute, and submission constraints.
