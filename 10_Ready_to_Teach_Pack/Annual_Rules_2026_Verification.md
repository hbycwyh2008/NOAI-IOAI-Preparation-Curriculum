# Annual Rules Verification — 2026

**Verified:** 2026-07-19  
**Scope:** annual IOAI facts and operational assumptions that may change. The current NOAI China handbook supplied by the organiser remains authoritative for the Chinese selection rounds.

## Confirmed current IOAI information

- IOAI 2026 is scheduled for **2–8 August 2026 in Astana, Republic of Kazakhstan**.
- The official 2026 syllabus classifies knowledge as **Theory**, **Practice**, or **Both**.
- The scientific contest design uses an educational **At-Home Round**, followed by two on-site individual contest stages. Contest 1 continues the at-home task families; Contest 2 introduces novel tasks.
- Official rules explicitly state that updates and technical clarifications may be issued before the event.
- The official Education Hub and the IOAI 2024/2025 task repositories are approved preparation sources.

## Curriculum rule

Annual official documents override this repository for:

- competition duration and submission limits;
- internet and website access;
- AI-assistant and external-API permissions;
- permitted pretrained models and local model assets;
- Python/package versions;
- GPU/CPU/storage limits;
- team, individual, GAITE, and Team Challenge formats;
- appeal and scoring procedures.

No lesson may tell students that a tool is allowed merely because it was allowed in an earlier year. In particular, 2025 environment and LLM rules are historical examples, not guaranteed 2026 rules.

## NOAI China local archive record

For the Chinese pathway, archive the organiser-supplied handbook/syllabus with:

- exact title and version;
- publication date;
- download/source location;
- Round 1 and Round 2 duration;
- allowed environment and tools;
- registration/eligibility rules;
- a change log against the previous version.

Do not infer a public download URL or redistribute a document unless permission is clear.
